GenOS Onboarding Experience

Streamlining Intuit’s internal GenAI development process

platform internal tool AI/ML enterprise

Overview

I designed the onboarding experience for Intuit’s GenOS — an internal GenAI system that powers products like QuickBooks, TurboTax, and Mailchimp. The goal was to help product teams easily onboard to the GenOS ecosystem and navigate reviews from legal, security, and AI experts without slowing down innovation.

Timeline 4 months

Problem

Context & Problem

Product teams across Intuit were beginning to build GenAI features, but onboarding to GenOS required navigating scattered resources, unclear processes, and multiple review steps. This complexity created confusion and delays for product teams and reviewers alike.

🛠️

Product Teams

Builders of GenAI features

📋

Reviewers

📊

Program Managers

Managers of GenAI use cases

The existing process took up to three weeks, involving countless back-and-forth messages and manual tracking in spreadsheets. Product teams didn’t know what steps to complete or when. Reviewers from Legal, Security, and AI teams lacked visibility into pending tasks, and program managers struggled to track hundreds of use cases across the company.

In short — everyone wanted to move faster, but the process wasn’t designed to scale.

Solution

Design Solution

I designed a centralized platform where teams could discover resources, complete onboarding steps, and track review progress — all in one place.

Key design decisions included:

  • Modular, non-linear onboarding: Allowed teams to complete steps in the order that matched their workflow while maintaining progress tracking.
  • Integrated dashboards: Displayed priorities, owners, and due dates for each review type to reduce back-and-forth communication.
  • Central resource hub: Consolidated documentation and templates so teams no longer needed to search across multiple systems.
  • Visibility for reviewers and program managers: Provided real-time tracking of all GenAI use cases, replacing manual spreadsheet management.
