GenAI Use Case Registry

Streamlining Intuit’s internal GenAI development process

GenAI internal tool governance enterprise

Overview

When generative AI emerged, Intuit needed to ship GenAI features fast to products like QuickBooks, TurboTax, and Mailchimp — but without a process to ensure legal, security, and AI reviews, the company could be exposed. I designed a centralized hub where product teams could register their GenAI use cases, connect to Intuit's GenAI capabilities,track review status, and get everything they needed to launch responsibly. More than 7.6k use cases have been registered through the platform.

Problem

GenAI was moving faster than the company's guardrails

In 2023, product teams across Intuit were racing to build GenAI features into TurboTax, QuickBooks, and other products. But there was no process for ensuring those features passed legal, security, and AI reviews before shipping. The consequences were real:

  • Product teams didn’t know who to contact for legal, security or AI sign-off, or even that they needed to.
  • Product teams wanted to move fast, but the extensive review processes were blocking them from being the pioneers in this new era.
  • Program managers responsible for tracking review status were doing it entirely in spreadsheets — manually chasing dozens of teams across Slack.
  • There was no single source of truth on the resources, guiding teams on what to complete and when.

My Process

Moving fast in ambiguous territory

This project had no clear precedent internally. GenAI governance frameworks were still being written industry-wide, and I was designing a system before the rules were fully defined. My first priority was understanding who was in pain and what the minimum viable process needed to look like.

Stakeholder mapping

I identified four distinct user groups with conflicting needs: product teams (wanted speed and clarity), program managers (needed visibility and control), legal reviewers (needed structured input, not ad-hoc requests), and security reviewers (needed consistent, complete submissions). Designing for all four without creating a bloated product was the central challenge.

Product Team Reviewers Program Managers Platform Team
Speed and clarity — ship GenAI features without guessing who to contact or what to do next. Structured, complete submissions from teams — not ad-hoc threads or half-filled requests. Visibility and control when tracking dozens or hundreds of use cases across the company. Self-serve onboarding to GenAI capabilities and guardrails teams can rely on at scale.

Discovery with program managers

The program managers were the most acute pain point. I ran working sessions with them to map their current process step by step — what they tracked, where information lived, how they followed up. This gave me a clear picture of what the system needed to replace.

The build vs. buy decision

One of the most consequential design decisions wasn't visual — it was architectural. The review form required product teams to answer detailed legal, security, and AI questions, update documents, and communicate through the comments in Google Docs with reviewers over time. Building this natively would have consumed months of engineering time we didn't have.

I evaluated third-party review tools and proposed integrating an existing platform via API rather than building the form experience from scratch. This let us ship the end-to-end experience in a fraction of the time, while the registry hub handled what only we could build: intake, resource access, status visibility, and configuration.

Solution

A central hub that connects the entire GenAI launch process

The registry wasn't a single screen — it was a system connecting four types of users through a shared workflow. Here's how it worked end to end.

  • Modular, non-linear onboarding: Allowed teams to complete steps in the order that matched their workflow while maintaining progress tracking.
  • Integrated dashboards: Displayed priorities, owners, and due dates for each review type to reduce back-and-forth communication.
  • Central resource hub: Consolidated documentation and templates so teams no longer needed to search across multiple systems.
  • Visibility for reviewers and program managers: Provided real-time tracking of all GenAI use cases, replacing manual spreadsheet management.
